Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert PDF pages to JPG images?
Upload your PDF, choose quality and format, then click Convert. The tool renders each PDF page using PDF.js and saves them as individual image files you can download.
What DPI should I choose?
For web use: Standard (72 DPI) is fine. For print or high quality: choose High (144 DPI) or Ultra (216 DPI). Higher DPI means larger file sizes but sharper images.
Can I convert only specific pages?
Yes! Select "Custom Range" and enter pages like "1-3, 5, 7-10". Only those pages will be converted and available for download.
PNG vs JPG — which should I choose?
Choose PNG if your PDF has transparent backgrounds or sharp text/diagrams you need at pixel-perfect quality. Choose JPG for smaller file sizes with photos or general content.
