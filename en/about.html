About Logrith

We built the tool platform we always wished existed — free, private, fast, and genuinely useful for everyone.

Our Mission

Logrith was built on a simple belief: the best tools should be free and accessible to everyone, regardless of technical skill, budget, or location. We saw people paying for subscriptions just to convert a JPG, or uploading sensitive documents to unknown servers just to merge a PDF. That felt wrong.

Our mission is to provide professional-grade digital tools that run entirely in your browser — no server uploads, no accounts, no paywalls, no watermarks. Just tools that work, instantly, for free.

What Makes Logrith Different

Most online tool sites upload your files to their servers for processing. This creates delays, privacy risks, and often requires paid plans for larger files. Logrith is architecturally different — every operation runs using modern browser APIs:

  • Image tools — HTML5 Canvas API (your images never leave your device)
  • PDF tools — PDF-Lib + PDF.js running entirely client-side
  • Crypto tools — Web Crypto API (same standard used by banks)
  • Text tools — Native JavaScript string processing, zero external calls
  • QR codes — QRCode.js library, generated locally in your browser

The result: instant processing, complete privacy, and functionality that works even with a poor internet connection after the initial page load.

Who Uses Logrith?

Freelancers & designers use Logrith to compress and convert images for client projects without expensive software subscriptions. Developers format JSON, generate secure passwords, test regex patterns, and compute hashes in their daily workflow. Students and researchers merge PDF documents, convert files between formats, and count words for essays. Small business owners generate QR codes for menus and marketing, calculate GST on invoices, and plan loan repayments with the EMI calculator. Content creators resize images for social media, generate Lorem Ipsum for mockups, and convert case for headlines.

Logrith is built for everyone who works with digital content — which, in 2026, is essentially everyone.

Our Technology Commitment

Logrith is built with plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no heavy frameworks, no bloated dependencies, no tracking scripts. Pages load fast on slow connections. Tools work on low-end devices. The codebase is intentionally lightweight so that anyone can inspect it and verify our privacy claims directly in their browser's developer tools.

We use established open-source libraries for complex operations: PDF-Lib for PDF manipulation, PDF.js from Mozilla for PDF rendering, and QRCode.js for QR generation. All other tools are written in vanilla JavaScript using native browser APIs.
